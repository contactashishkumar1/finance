Building Sustainable Passive Income in 2026

Passive income is the "holy grail" of personal finance. It is money that flows into your bank account without your active participation in a job or business. In the Indian economy of 2026, creating multiple streams of passive income is no longer a luxury but a necessity to combat inflation and job market volatility.

The Passive Income Calculator helps you transition from an "Earn-Spend" cycle to an "Invest-Harvest" cycle. By defining your target monthly payout, you can reverse-engineer the required corpus and the monthly SIP behavior needed to reach that milestone.

The 3 Best Passive Income Sources in India

To reach the numbers shown in our calculator, modern Indian investors typically use a combination of these three buckets:

  • Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP): Investing in Equity Mutual Funds and withdrawing a fixed amount monthly. This is the most tax-efficient method for high-growth portfolios.
  • Dividend Portfolios: Owning shares of high-quality, dividend-paying companies (PSUs, Utilities, Blue-chips). This provides a natural cash flow that grows with corporate profits.
  • Fixed Income (Bonds/FDs): Providing a stable, low-risk foundation. While yields are lower (around 6-8%), the stability is critical for your base expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. Can I live entirely on passive income?
Yes, once your passive income exceeds your monthly expenses, you have achieved Financial Independence. Most FIRE practitioners aim for a corpus of 25x-30x their annual expenses to ensure sustainability.
2. How is passive income taxed in India?
Dividends and Interest are added to your income and taxed at your slab rate. Rental income gets a flat 30% deduction before tax. SWP payouts from equity funds are highly tax-efficient, with only gains taxed at 12.5% (LTCG).
3. Is real estate good for passive income?
Real estate offers rental yield (2-3% for residential, 7-9% for commercial) plus capital appreciation. However, it requires active management and has high maintenance. REITs are a better "passive" way to invest in real estate.
4. What is a "Safe Withdrawal Rate"?
It is the percentage of your portfolio you can withdraw annually without ever running out of money. The classic rule is 4%, but in India, due to higher inflation, many advisors recommend 3% to 3.5% for 30+ years of retirement.
