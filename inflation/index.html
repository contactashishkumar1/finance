What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising. As inflation grows, every rupee you own buys a smaller percentage of a good or service. This process is essentially the erosion of purchasing power.

In an Indian context, if the inflation rate is 6%, an item that costs ₹100 today will cost ₹106 next year. While this seems small in the short term, over 20-30 years, it can quadruple the cost of education, healthcare, and daily living. Why Inflation Matters for Financial Planning

Failing to account for inflation is the biggest mistake in long-term financial planning. Here is why it matters:

  • Retirement Planning: ₹1 Lakh monthly expense today might require ₹4 Lakhs monthly in 25 years just to maintain the same standard of living.
  • Savings Goals: If your Fixed Deposit earns 7% interest but inflation is 6%, your "Real Rate of Return" is only 1%.
  • Investment Strategy: To beat inflation, investors often need to look beyond traditional savings and explore Mutual Fund SIPs or Equity.

Inflation Formula Used in the Calculator

The calculator uses the compound interest formula to determine the future cost of goods:

FV = PV × (1 + r)n

FV: Future Value (Cost in the future)

PV: Present Value (Current amount/cost)

r: Annual Inflation Rate (decimal)

n: Number of Years

Example of Inflation Calculation

Let's take a common goal: Higher Education. If a course costs ₹10,00,000 today, what will it cost in 15 years assuming a 6% average inflation rate?

Calculation: 1,000,000 × (1 + 0.06)15 = ₹23,96,558

This means in just 15 years, the cost has more than doubled. This is why using a Future Value Inflation Calculator is vital for parenting and retirement goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What inflation rate should I assume in India?
For long-term planning (10-20 years), assuming an inflation rate of 6% is standard for India. For specific sectors like Education or Healthcare, inflation can be as high as 8-10%.
How accurate are inflation calculators?
They are mathematically accurate based on your inputs. However, actual inflation fluctuates due to government policy, global oil prices, and monsoon performance.
Does inflation affect all expenses equally?
No. Electronics often see "deflation" or stable prices, while services like education, housing, and domestic help usually grow faster than the average CPI inflation rate.
