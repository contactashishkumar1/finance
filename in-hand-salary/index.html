How is In-Hand Salary Calculated?

Net Salary = Gross Salary – (EPF + Income Tax + Professional Tax)

Gross Salary: CTC minus Employer's contribution (PF).

Employee PF: Usually 12% of Basic Salary contributed by the employee.

Income Tax: Calculated based on New Tax Regime slabs (FY 25-26) on the Gross amount after standard deductions.

Example Breakdown (₹10 LPA CTC)

For a CTC of ₹10,00,000:
  • Monthly CTC: ₹83,333
  • Employer PF Portion: ~₹5,000
  • Monthly Gross (Cash Component): ~₹78,333
  • Employee PF Portion: ~₹5,000
  • Net In-Hand (after Rebate): ~₹73,133

What is In-Hand Salary?

In-hand salary, also known as net take-home pay, is the amount an employee receives after all statutory and voluntary deductions are subtracted from the Gross Salary. While the CTC (Cost to Company) looks high on an offer letter, the actual cash flow depends on several factors like EPF, TDS, and PT.

Understanding this breakup is crucial for budgeting and financial planning. If you are comparing two job offers, always look at the in-hand component rather than just the CTC. CTC vs Gross vs Net Salary

Distinguishing between CTC, Gross, and Net is essential for anyone expecting an appraisal or a new job. Use our Salary Hike Calculator to predict your future earnings.

  • CTC: The total cost a company incurs on an employee. It includes basic, HRA, bonuses, employer PF, insurance, and sometimes even office space costs.
  • Gross Salary: CTC minus Employer's PF and statutory employer contributions. It is the taxable base before employee-side deductions.
  • Net (In-Hand) Salary: Gross salary minus Income Tax (TDS), Employee's PF contribution, and Professional Tax. This is what is credited to your bank.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is in-hand salary for ₹10 LPA?
For ₹10 LPA CTC, the in-hand salary is approximately ₹73,000 - ₹75,000 per month. This assumes Employer and Employee PF deductions and zero income tax due to the ₹12L rebate in the new regime.
How is PF calculated on salary?
Employee PF is usually 12% of Basic Salary. In a CTC model, the employer also contributes 12%, which is deducted from the total CTC to reach the Gross Salary.
How to increase in-hand salary?
You can opt for the New Tax Regime if it offers lower tax. Also, structure salary components like 'Special Allowance' or 'Flexi Benefits' to manage taxable components. Use our Salary Hike Calculator for appraisal planning.
What is Section 87A Rebate?
Section 87A provides a rebate making your tax zero if taxable income is up to ₹12 Lakhs in the New Regime (FY 2025-26).
What is the difference between CTC and take-home salary?
CTC (Cost to Company) is the total amount the company spends on you, including perks and employer PF. Take-home salary is what remains after statutory deductions like PF and Income Tax.
Is standard deduction available in the new tax regime?
Yes, a standard deduction of ₹75,000 is available for all salaried individuals under the New Tax Regime for FY 2025-26.
What is Professional Tax (PT)?
Professional Tax is a small monthly deduction (usually ₹200) mandated by specific state governments in India. It is deducted from your gross salary to reach the net in-hand figure.

Quick Summary

• CTC includes Employer PF; Gross Salary excludes it.

• Net Salary is what you receive after Employee PF and Income Tax.

• The New Regime is highly beneficial for income up to ₹12 Lakhs.
