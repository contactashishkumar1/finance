The 10% Rule: Why Education Inflation is Higher

While the Consumer Price Index (CPI) in India often hovers around 5-6%, Education Inflation is consistently higher at 10-12%. This is due to the rising costs of technology integration, international faculty exchange programs, and the increasing demand for high-quality tertiary education.

For example, an Engineering degree that costs ₹15 Lakhs in 2026 will likely cost nearly ₹63 Lakhs by 2040. If you don't start saving early, you may be forced to rely on heavy debt. Check our Education Loan Moratorium Tool to see how much a student loan will cost if you don't build a fund today.

Strategy: SSY vs. Mutual Fund SIP

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

  • Returns: ~8.2% (Government Fixed)
  • Tax: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt)
  • Limit: ₹1.5 Lakhs annually.
  • Verdict: Safe, but usually fails to beat 10% education inflation.

Equity Mutual Fund SIP

  • Returns: 12-15% (Market Linked)
  • Tax: 12.5% LTCG on gains > ₹1.25L.
  • Limit: No upper limit.
  • Verdict: Volatile but essential to reach ₹1 Cr+ targets over 15 years.

Parental Planning FAQs

Why is education inflation 10%?
Education in India operates on a specialized cost index. Premium institutes often adjust fees annually to cover growing administrative, faculty, and technology costs.
Is ₹50 Lakhs enough for higher education in 2040?
For a Tier-1 Indian MBA, it might barely suffice. For Medical or Overseas education, ₹50L will likely be insufficient as costs are projected to cross ₹1.5 Cr to ₹2.5 Cr.
Should I use Gold for education?
Gold acts as a currency hedge and inflation buffer. While it shouldn't be your only asset, holding 10-15% in SGBs or Gold ETFs can protect your child's overseas education from rupee depreciation.
What is a Step-up SIP?
A Step-up SIP increases your monthly contribution by a fixed % (e.g., 10%) every year as your salary increases. This is the most efficient way to hit high education targets. Use our Step-Up SIP tool to plan this.
Can I withdraw from my EPF?
Yes, you can withdraw up to 50% of your EPF contribution for a child's higher education after 7 years of service. However, this impacts your retirement corpus. It's better to build a dedicated education SIP.
Average cost of MBA in 2026?
A premier 2-year MBA in India currently costs between ₹25 Lakhs to ₹32 Lakhs. This includes tuition, hostel, and study materials.
