What is a Personal Loan EMI?

A Personal Loan EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed monthly payment that you make to a lender to repay an unsecured loan. Unlike a Home Loan, a personal loan doesn't require collateral, which is why the interest rates are generally higher, typically ranging between 10.5% and 24% p.a. in India.

The EMI stays constant throughout the tenure, helping you plan your monthly budget. In the early stages of the loan, a larger part of your EMI goes toward paying off the interest. Over time, as the principal reduces, more of the payment goes toward clearing the balance. Use our Loan Eligibility Tool to see if you qualify.

How Personal Loan EMI is Calculated

Financial institutions use an amortized reducing balance formula to calculate EMIs. The mathematical logic is as follows:

EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]
  • P: Principal Loan Amount.
  • r: Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100).
  • n: Loan Tenure in Months (Years × 12).

Tips to Reduce Your Personal Loan Burden

Shorter Tenure

While it increases monthly EMI, it drastically cuts the total interest payout. Aim for 3 years instead of 5 if possible.

Early Prepayment

Any extra payment directly reduces the principal. In India, check if your bank charges a foreclosure penalty before prepaying.

Balance Transfer

If your CIBIL score has improved, switch to a lender offering a 2-3% lower rate to reduce your long-term cost.

Invest Early

Better yet, use your SIP savings to avoid high-interest loans entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between personal and home loans?
Personal loans are unsecured (no collateral) and have higher interest rates. Home loans are secured by the property and offer much lower rates along with tax benefits.
How can I reduce my monthly EMI?
You can reduce monthly EMI by increasing the tenure, choosing a lower interest lender, or making a lump-sum principal prepayment.
Can my EMI change during the tenure?
Yes, if you have a floating rate loan, your EMI or tenure will change if the RBI Repo Rate or the bank's MCLR changes.
Does prepayment help in personal loans?
Yes. Prepaying even small amounts reduces the principal, saving you massive interest in the long run. Check for foreclosure charges before doing so.
What credit score is needed for a personal loan?
A CIBIL score of 750 or higher is considered ideal. It helps in getting faster approvals and the most competitive interest rates from Indian banks.
What are typical personal loan interest rates?
In 2026, rates typically range from 10.5% (Public banks) to 21% (Private NBFCs). Salary and company category play a major role in the final offer.
