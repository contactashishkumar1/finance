How is Future Education Cost Calculated?

FV = PV × (1 + r)n

FV: Future Cost of Education | PV: Current Cost

r: Yearly Inflation Rate | n: Years to Start

Why Education Planning is Critical in India?

Education inflation in India is significantly higher than general inflation, often touching double digits. A professional degree that costs ₹10 Lakhs today could easily exceed ₹40 Lakhs in 15 years. Without a dedicated Education Planning Calculator, most parents underestimate the required corpus, leading to heavy education loans later.

Planning early allows you to leverage the power of compounding. By investing in equity mutual funds or Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (for girls), you can build a massive corpus with small monthly contributions. Our calculator helps you bridge the gap between current affordability and future necessity.

Education Planning Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. Should I consider foreign education in my plan?
Yes, if you aspire for your child to study abroad, you must account for currency depreciation (USD/INR) alongside education inflation. Usually, adding an extra 3-4% to inflation covers this.
2. How much should I invest monthly?
This depends on the current cost of the degree and the time left. Use our Education Planning Calculator above to get the exact monthly SIP amount based on your specific requirements.
3. What is the impact of taxes on education savings?
Most long-term capital gains (LTCG) in equity are taxed at 12.5% (above ₹1.25 Lakh). You should ideally target a corpus that is 10-15% higher than your actual goal to account for future taxes.
4. Should I choose an Education Loan or save for college?
Saving in advance is always cheaper as you earn interest on your savings, whereas a loan requires you to pay high interest (usually 9-12% p.a.). Education loans should only be a fallback for gaps in funding.
5. How does currency depreciation affect foreign education planning?
When planning for overseas education, you must account for Rupee depreciation (historically 3-4% p.a. against the USD) in addition to university tuition inflation.
6. How to save for a child's education in India?
The best way is to start a Goal-based SIP in diversified equity mutual funds. For a girl child, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is an excellent debt-based option with tax benefits.
7. What are the tax-saving options for child education?
Under Section 80C, you can claim tax deductions for school/college tuition fees. For investments, SSY offers EEE status, while ELSS mutual funds offer 80C benefits during the accumulation phase.
