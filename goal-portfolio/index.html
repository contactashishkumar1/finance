How is Goal SIP Calculated?

SIP = (Goal - L(1+i)n) / [((1+i)n - 1)/i × (1+i)]

Goal: Future Target Amount | L: Current Savings (Lumpsum)

i: Monthly ROI Rate (Annual / 12 / 100)

n: Time in Months (Years × 12)

Why Goal-Based Portfolio Planning Matters?

Traditional investing often focuses on "returns," but strategic investing focuses on "targets." The **Goal Based Portfolio Planner** is designed to shift your mindset from merely saving money to funding specific milestones. In the Indian economy of 2026, where costs for education and property are rising faster than general inflation, having a fixed target is the only way to ensure financial security.

This planner uses a reverse-engineering approach. Instead of telling you what your ₹5,000 SIP will become, it asks what you need (e.g., ₹1 Crore for retirement) and tells you the exact investment behavior required today. By incorporating your existing savings, it acknowledges that most investors aren't starting from zero.

The Concept of SMART Financial Goals

For this planner to be effective, your goals should be:

  • Specific: Don't just save for "the future"—save for a "₹20 Lakh car."
  • Measurable: Assign a hard currency value to your objective.
  • Achievable: Use our calculator to see if your required SIP fits your monthly income.
  • Relevant: Ensure the goal aligns with your long-term life values.
  • Time-bound: Set a clear deadline (e.g., 10 years).

Goal Planning Frequently Asked Questions

1. Should I include my emergency fund in 'Current Savings'?
No. Your emergency fund (6 months of expenses) is a safety net and should never be used for long-term goal funding. Only enter the savings you are willing to lock away for the goal's duration.
2. How do I factor in inflation?
Inflation makes things more expensive in the future. To reach a "₹50 Lakh value" in 10 years, you should ideally target a ₹80-90 Lakh corpus to maintain the same purchasing power.
3. What is a realistic ROI for a 10-year goal in India?
For a diversified equity mutual fund portfolio, 12% is a conservative long-term average. For hybrid portfolios, expect 9-10%, and for purely debt-based goals, 7-8%.
4. Can I reach ₹1 Crore in 20 years with ₹5,000 SIP?
Yes, if you get a 12% ROI, a ₹10,100 SIP reaches ₹1 Crore. However, with just ₹5,000, you would reach about ₹49 Lakhs. To hit ₹1 Crore with ₹5,000, you would need to use a 'Step-Up' strategy of increasing your SIP by 10% annually.
5. Does this planner account for taxes?
The calculator provides pre-tax maturity values. For equity gains exceeding ₹1.25 Lakh per year, a 12.5% Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax applies as of 2026. You should target a 10% higher corpus to cover taxes.
6. What if my ROI is lower than expected?
This is why annual reviews are important. If the market underperforms, you must either increase your monthly SIP or extend your goal timeline by a year or two.
7. Can I plan for multiple goals simultaneously?
Yes, though it is better to calculate them separately or use our Multiple Goal Planner to see your combined monthly commitment.
8. Is Goal SIP better than Lumpsum?
Both are good. Lumpsum is great for starting early with a large amount, while SIP ensures you continue to buy units across all market cycles (Rupee Cost Averaging). This planner combines both for the best result.
