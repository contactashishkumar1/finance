How is Property ROI Calculated?

Total ROI % = [(Gain from Value + Total Rent) / Total Cost] × 100

Total Cost: Purchase Price + Acquisition Taxes + Repairs. Check your Net Worth for the full portfolio view.

Net Gain: Current Value + Total Rent - Total Cost. For annualized growth, use the CAGR Tool.

Annualized ROI: Measures the geometric progression of your real estate wealth.

The Real Cost of Real Estate: Calculating True ROI

Real estate is one of the most popular asset classes in India, but it is also the most misunderstood in terms of actual returns. Most investors only look at the "Buy Price" vs. the "Sell Price." However, a professional Property ROI Calculator must account for the "leakages" (costs) and the "bonuses" (rental income) to find the true Internal Rate of Return. For irregular cashflows, use our XIRR tool.

In 2026, with the Indian property market maturing, capital appreciation alone may not beat high-growth equities. The difference between a "good" and an "average" real estate investment lies in the rental yield. A property with a 3% rental yield and 7% appreciation provides a 10% gross return—but when you subtract costs, the real return might be lower.

The Dual Engines of Property Return

1. Capital Appreciation: This is the increase in the market price of your property over time. For accurate growth forecasting, use our Property Appreciation Tool.

2. Rental Yield: This is the recurring income you earn. If you're building a portfolio from zero, a high-yield SIP might help you accumulate the downpayment for a property faster.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Should I include registration and stamp duty in ROI?
Absolutely. Your ROI is based on your "total capital outlay." If you buy a house for ₹1 Crore but pay ₹7 Lakhs in stamp duty and ₹1 Lakh in brokerage, your ROI should be calculated from a ₹1.08 Crore base.
2. How does a home loan affect my ROI?
Loans provide "leverage." You can control a high-value asset with a small down payment. If the property appreciates, your return on your *own* capital is amplified. However, high interest rates can eat into this profit.
3. What is Unrealized ROI?
If you haven't sold the property, your appreciation is "unrealized." It exists on paper based on current market trends. Rental income is "realized" ROI because it is actual cash in hand.
4. Does ROI include income tax?
Most ROI calculators show pre-tax returns. In India, you must account for 12.5% LTCG on capital gains and your respective slab rate for rental income.
5. Why is CAGR better than Absolute ROI?
Absolute ROI doesn't consider time. A 50% profit over 2 years is 22% CAGR, but 50% profit over 10 years is only 4% CAGR. CAGR helps you compare property against assets like FDs.
6. What is a good real estate ROI in 2026?
A combined ROI (Rent + Appreciation) of 10-12% is considered healthy in the Indian market. Higher returns are possible in high-demand micro-markets.
7. Does maintenance affect ROI?
Yes. Recurring costs like maintenance, society charges, and property tax directly reduce your net rental income, thereby lowering your overall ROI.
8. Can I use this for land/plots?
Yes. For plots, simply set the monthly rent to zero. The ROI will be based purely on capital appreciation minus holding costs (property tax, fencing).
