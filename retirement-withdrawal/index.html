Sustainability of Your Retirement Fund

Planning for retirement is not just about accumulating a large corpus; it is primarily about determining how that corpus will serve you through your non-earning years. The Retirement Withdrawal Calculator is an essential tool to visualize the "decumulation" phase of your financial journey. To build your corpus effectively, use our SIP or Lumpsum tools.

In the Indian context, where inflation remains relatively higher than in developed markets, the rate at which you withdraw from your fund is critical. If your withdrawal rate exceeds the interest generation rate of your portfolio, you will eventually begin to eat into your principal. While this is expected in later stages of retirement, doing it too early can lead to outliving your money. Check the impact of price rises with our Inflation Tool.

The Role of the 4% Rule

Historically, financial planners have suggested the "4% Rule," which states that if you withdraw 4% of your total corpus in the first year and adjust that amount for inflation subsequently, your money is likely to last at least 30 years. However, with changing interest rates and medical inflation, many advisors in 2026 suggest a more conservative **3.5% withdrawal rate** for Indian retirees. Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a safe retirement withdrawal rate in India?
While the global '4% rule' is popular, in India, a safe withdrawal rate is often considered to be between 3% to 4.5%, depending on inflation and the portfolio's equity exposure.
2. How does inflation affect retirement withdrawals?
Inflation increases the cost of living, meaning you need to withdraw more money each year to maintain the same lifestyle, which can deplete your corpus faster.
3. How is withdrawal tax handled in India?
Withdrawals from mutual funds (SWP) are taxed as capital gains, whereas withdrawals from FDs or NPS are usually taxed as per your income tax slab.
4. Can I use the bucket strategy for withdrawals?
Yes, dividing your corpus into immediate (Cash), mid-term (Debt), and long-term (Equity) buckets helps manage volatility and ensures the fund lasts longer.
5. What happens if the market crashes early in retirement?
This is known as 'Sequence of Returns Risk.' A crash in early years can drastically shorten fund longevity. Keeping a 2-year cash buffer is recommended.
6. Should I reinvest my pension into an SWP?
Moving lumpsum pension amounts into a Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) in a hybrid fund can provide better post-tax real returns compared to traditional annuities.
7. How does ROI impact the fund duration?
Every 1% increase in annual returns can extend your fund's life by several years. However, higher ROI usually requires higher equity exposure and risk.
8. Is medical inflation different from general inflation?
Yes, in India, medical inflation often ranges between 12-14%, which is double the general CPI inflation. Retirees should account for this separately.
