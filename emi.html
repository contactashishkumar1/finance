How is Loan EMI Calculated?

E = P × r × (1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n - 1)

E: EMI Amount | P: Principal Loan Amount

r: Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)

n: Loan Tenure in Months

EMI Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. What is an EMI and how is it calculated?
EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It consists of a portion of the principal and a portion of the interest. It is calculated using a standard formula based on loan amount, rate, and tenure.
2. Does a longer tenure reduce the EMI amount?
Yes, increasing the loan tenure reduces the monthly EMI. However, it significantly increases the total interest you pay over the life of the loan.
3. Can I reduce my EMI mid-tenure?
Yes, usually through two ways: making a significant part-prepayment to reduce the principal, or opting for a loan balance transfer to a bank offering a lower interest rate.
4. What is a Reducing Balance Interest Rate?
It means interest is calculated only on the remaining loan balance. As you pay back the principal monthly, the interest component decreases, making it cheaper than a flat interest rate.
5. Is there a penalty for prepaying a loan?
Most floating-rate Home Loans in India have zero prepayment charges as per RBI rules. However, fixed-rate loans and personal loans may have a penalty fee (usually 2-4%).
6. How does CIBIL score affect my EMI?
A high CIBIL score (above 750) qualifies you for the lowest market interest rates. A lower score might result in a higher interest rate, which directly increases your EMI amount.
7. What are the tax benefits of Home Loan EMIs?
Under Section 80C, you can claim up to ₹1.5 Lakh for principal repayment. Under Section 24(b), you can claim up to ₹2 Lakh for interest repayment annually on self-occupied property.
8. Fixed vs Floating rates: which is better?
Floating rates change with the RBI repo rate and are usually lower. Fixed rates stay constant throughout but are usually 1-2% higher than floating rates.
