What is Equated Monthly Installment (EMI)?

Understanding EMI

An EMI is a fixed amount of money that a borrower pays to a lender at a specific date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both the interest and principal of a loan over a period of years until the loan is paid off in full.

In the initial years, a larger portion of your EMI goes toward paying interest. As the loan matures, more of the payment is applied toward the principal.

The Reducing Balance Method

Most Indian banks use the "Reducing Balance" method. Here, interest is calculated on the outstanding loan amount at the end of every month.

"Making prepayments early in your loan tenure can drastically reduce your total interest burden and shorten your tenure."

Factors Affecting Your Loan EMI

Principal Amount

The base amount you borrow. Higher principal leads to higher EMIs.

Interest Rate

Directly impacts the 'cost' of the loan. Lower rates mean lower EMIs.

Tenure

Longer tenures reduce EMI but increase total interest paid.

Loan EMI Frequently Asked Questions

Can I reduce my EMI mid-tenure in India?
Yes. You can reduce your EMI by making a partial prepayment or by asking your bank to extend the loan tenure. However, extending tenure increases your total interest cost.
What are the tax benefits on Home Loan EMI?
Under the Old Tax Regime, you can claim up to ₹2 Lakh on interest (Section 24) and up to ₹1.5 Lakh on principal (Section 80C) annually. These benefits vary depending on the tax regime chosen.
Fixed vs Floating interest rate: which is better?
Floating rates are usually lower and change with market conditions (Repo Rate). Fixed rates stay constant but are typically higher. Most home loans in India are floating-rate loans.
Is it better to pay off a loan early?
Generally, yes. Prepaying reduces the principal, which significantly lowers the interest burden. Most floating-rate home loans in India have zero prepayment penalties, but personal loans might have them.
How much of my salary should go into EMI?
Financial experts suggest that all your EMIs combined should ideally not exceed 40-50% of your net monthly take-home pay to maintain a healthy financial lifestyle.
Does my credit score affect my EMI?
Absolutely. A high CIBIL score (usually 750+) can help you negotiate a lower interest rate with the bank, which directly reduces your monthly EMI amount.
What is a loan moratorium period?
A moratorium is a period during which you are not required to pay EMIs (e.g., during a crisis or for student loans). Note that interest usually still accrues during this period, increasing your total debt.
Can I get a loan without a credit card?
Yes. While credit cards help build history, you can get a loan based on your income, employment stability, and other banking history. "No Cost EMI" on debit cards is also popular in India now.
What are typical loan processing fees?
In India, processing fees usually range from 0.5% to 2% of the loan amount. Many banks waive these fees during festive seasons or for premium account holders.
What is an amortization schedule?
It is a table showing each EMI payment over the entire tenure, detailing how much of each payment goes toward principal versus interest, and the remaining balance after each payment.
Does GST apply on EMI payments?
GST is not applicable on the EMI (Principal + Interest) itself. However, it is charged (currently 18%) on service-related fees like processing fees, documentation charges, or late payment penalties.
What happens if I miss an EMI payment?
Missing an EMI can lead to late payment fees, a significant drop in your credit score, and potentially legal action or asset repossession if the default continues. It is always best to inform the bank if you're facing a crisis.
