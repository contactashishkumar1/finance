Step-by-Step Formula:

The division by 26 represents the number of working days in a month, and the multiplication by 15 signifies half a month's salary for every year worked.

What is Gratuity?

Gratuity is a monetary benefit provided by an employer to an employee in recognition of their long-term service to the organization. It is a defined benefit plan governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972.

Key Eligibility Criteria

  • ✅ Min Service: Must complete 5 years of continuous service.
  • ✅ Rounding Rule: Service over 6 months in a year is rounded to the next year.
  • ✅ Limit: The statutory maximum tax-free limit is ₹20 Lakh.

Is Gratuity Taxable?

Government Employees

Gratuity received by any government employee (Central, State, or Local Authority) is completely tax-free without any upper limit.

Private Sector Employees

For employees covered under the Gratuity Act, the least of the following is exempt from tax: 1) ₹20 Lakh, 2) Actual Gratuity received, or 3) Eligible Gratuity amount. You can estimate the taxable portion using our Tax tool.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum service required for gratuity?
An employee must complete at least 5 years of continuous service with the same employer to become eligible for gratuity. However, this is waived in case of death or disablement of the employee.
How is gratuity calculated in India?
It is calculated as (Last Drawn Salary × 15 × Years of Service) / 26. Salary here specifically means Basic Salary plus Dearness Allowance (DA). This tool automates this calculation instantly.
Is gratuity taxable?
For private sector employees, gratuity is tax-exempt up to ₹20 Lakh. Any amount received over this limit is taxable as per your income tax slab. You can estimate tax on gratuity using our Income Tax Calculator.
What is the maximum gratuity limit in India?
The current maximum tax-free limit for gratuity is ₹20 Lakh for private sector employees. Employers can choose to pay more than this, but the excess will be taxable.
