What is Dividend Growth Investing?

Dividend growth investing is a long-term strategy focused on purchasing shares of companies that not only pay regular dividends but also increase their dividend payouts over time. This approach prioritizes growing passive income over pure capital appreciation, although high-quality dividend growers often see their stock prices rise as well.

In the Indian market, companies like TCS, Infosys, and HDFC Bank have historical track records of dividend payouts. By using a "DRIP" (Dividend Reinvestment Plan) strategy, investors can accelerate their compounding. Frequently Asked Questions

What is dividend yield?
It's the ratio of a company's annual dividend compared to its share price. A ₹10 dividend on a ₹100 stock is a 10% yield.
How does dividend growth work?
Companies that increase their earnings often raise their dividend payouts. For example, if a dividend grows 10% annually, your income doubles every ~7 years.
Is reinvestment important?
Reinvesting allows you to buy more shares without adding new capital, which creates a 'snowball effect' on your total income.
What is DRIP?
DRIP stands for Dividend Reinvestment Plan. It automates the process of buying additional shares with the cash dividends you receive.
Best dividend strategy?
Focus on companies with strong free cash flow and a payout ratio below 60% to ensure the dividend is sustainable and can grow.
Can dividends replace salary?
Yes, but it requires a high savings rate and long-term consistency. At a 4% yield, you'd need 25x your annual expenses in your portfolio.
Are dividends taxable in India?
Yes, they are added to your total income and taxed according to your slab. Keep this in mind when calculating "Net" passive income.

Strategic Summary

• Dividend growth provides a hedge against inflation by increasing your purchasing power annually.

• Reinvesting dividends (DRIP) can more than triple your final portfolio value over 20 years.

• Focus on 'Yield on Cost'—the return relative to your initial investment—to see true compounding success.
