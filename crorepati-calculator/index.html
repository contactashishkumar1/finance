What is a Crorepati Calculator?

A Crorepati Calculator is a financial planning tool that helps investors estimate the timeline required to accumulate a corpus of ₹1 Crore (or any custom target). It accounts for your existing savings and your monthly Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) contributions.

Building a corpus of ₹1 Crore is a common milestone for Indian investors. By using this tool, you can determine if your current savings rate is sufficient to reach your goal or if you need to increase your SIP investment to meet your desired deadline.

How SIP Can Help You Become a Crorepati

The secret to reaching ₹1 Crore isn't just a high income; it's the Power of Compounding. When you invest monthly, you earn returns on your principal and on the returns of previous months.

Ex The Path to ₹1.8 Crore

  • Monthly SIP: ₹10,000
  • Expected Returns: 12% p.a.
  • Duration: 25 Years
  • Total Invested: ₹30,00,000
  • Maturity Wealth: ₹1,89,76,351

Tips to Become a Crorepati Faster

Start Early

The longer your money stays in the market, the harder compounding works. Starting at 25 vs 35 can make a ₹2 Cr difference.

Step Up Your SIP

Increasing your SIP by just 10% every year as your salary grows can cut your timeline by 5-7 years. Use Savings Goal planning.

Avoid Withdrawals

Redeeming units for short-term needs resets the compounding clock. Keep your crorepati fund separate from your emergency fund.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become a crorepati?
It depends on your investment. A ₹10,000 SIP at 12% interest takes about 20 years. A ₹25,000 SIP at the same rate takes only 13 years.
Can I reach ₹1 Crore with ₹5,000 monthly?
Yes, but it takes time. At a 12% return rate, it will take approximately 26 years of disciplined investing to reach ₹1 Crore.
Is ₹1 Crore enough for retirement?
While ₹1 Crore is a great milestone, its value in 20 years will be lower due to inflation. Check our Retirement Calculator to see your inflation-adjusted needs.
Which is the best investment to reach ₹1 Crore?
Equity Mutual Funds or Index Funds are historically the most preferred instruments in India for reaching ₹1 Crore as they offer higher potential for long-term compounding compared to FDs.
