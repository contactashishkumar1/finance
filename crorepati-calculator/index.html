Reaching a corpus of ₹1 Crore is a common milestone for Indian investors. Using a SIP Calculator, you can visualize how small monthly contributions compound over decades. For example, to hit the ₹1 Crore mark at a 12% return rate:

  • ₹5,000 monthly: Takes approximately 26 years.
  • ₹15,000 monthly: Takes approximately 18 years.
  • ₹50,000 monthly: Takes approximately 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become a crorepati?
It depends on your SIP amount and returns. For example, a ₹15,000 monthly SIP at 15% returns will reach ₹1 Crore in 15 years.
What is the 15-15-15 rule?
The 15-15-15 rule states that an investment of ₹15,000 per month for 15 years at an interest rate of 15% results in a corpus of ₹1 Crore.
How much SIP needed for ₹1 crore?
At 12% ROI, ₹10,000 monthly for 20 years or ₹28,500 monthly for 12 years will get you to the ₹1 Crore goal.
Is ₹1 crore enough in 20 years?
Due to inflation (averaging 6% in India), ₹1 Crore in 20 years will be equivalent to about ₹31 Lakhs in today's money. Always plan for inflation.
What return assumption should I use?
For 10+ year horizons, 12% is a conservative and safe equity assumption for the Indian stock market.
