Why Financial Calculations Matter

A significant majority of investment decisions are made based on historical returns or emotional bias without a fundamental understanding of the underlying mathematics. Financial planning is essentially an exercise in precision where small variables can lead to vastly different outcomes over long durations.

Small differences in interest rates, compounding frequencies, or loan tenures often result in wealth differences amounting to lakhs of rupees. Without calculating these impacts beforehand, individuals risk over-leveraging their income or underestimating the corpus required for future goals.

How Money Grows

01

Compounding

Compounding is the process where interest earned on an investment is reinvested to earn further interest. Over 10-20 years, the "interest on interest" component often exceeds the original principal deposited.

02

Time Value of Money

A rupee today is worth more than a rupee tomorrow due to inflation. Understanding the future value of current savings is critical to ensuring that a financial goal remains achievable in the future economy.

03

Interest Rate Impact

A mere 1% difference in annual returns can change the final maturity value by several lakhs over a 20-year SIP. Precision in choosing the right asset class is therefore paramount.

If You Only Do 3 Financial Calculations in Life

EMI Affordability Rule

Your total monthly EMIs should ideally stay below **30% of your monthly take-home income**. Over-leveraging leads to financial stress and limits your ability to save for future goals.

SIP Goal Planning

Never invest a random surplus. Always **calculate the required SIP amount** needed to achieve a specific target corpus based on realistic expected returns and tenure.

FD Reality Check

A Fixed Deposit's attractiveness should only be judged after subtracting **income tax and prevailing inflation** from the nominal interest rate. This reveals the "Real Rate of Return".

Investing vs Borrowing Decisions

SIP vs FD: While Fixed Deposits offer guaranteed returns and capital protection, they often fail to beat inflation effectively. SIPs offer market-linked growth which is essential for wealth creation over 10+ years.

EMI Affordability: A lower EMI often tempts borrowers into longer tenures. However, a 20-year loan typically results in the borrower paying nearly double the principal in interest alone. Shortening the tenure is the most effective way to reduce debt cost.

Planning Strategy: Financial success is achieved by balancing high-growth assets (SIP) with secure assets (FD), while keeping borrowing costs (EMI) at a minimum level relative to monthly income.
