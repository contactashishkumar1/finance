Understanding Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

What is an SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Instead of a large one-time lumpsum investment, SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, or weekly).

It follows the concept of "Time in the market is more important than timing the market."

Why choose Step-up SIP?

As your income grows every year, your savings should too. A Step-up SIP automatically increases your contribution by a fixed percentage or amount annually.

"Even a 10% annual increase in SIP can lead to a significantly larger corpus over 20 years compared to a flat SIP."

Benefits of Investing via SIP

Discipline

Instills a habit of regular saving and investing.

Compounding

Earn interest on your interest over the long term.

Rupee Cost Averaging

Buy more units when markets are low.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SIP better than FD in India?
While FD offers guaranteed safety, Equity SIPs in India typically provide much higher inflation-beating returns (12-15%) over 5+ years. SIPs are ideal for wealth creation, whereas FDs are better for short-term capital safety.
What happens if I stop SIP midway?
If you stop midway, your existing units remain invested and continue to earn market returns. There is no penalty from the fund house for stopping, though your bank might charge for a missed auto-debit if not cancelled properly.
Can I withdraw SIP anytime?
Yes, most SIPs (Open-ended funds) allow withdrawal anytime. However, ELSS (Tax Saving) funds have a 3-year lock-in. Note that withdrawing within 1 year may attract an Exit Load (usually 1%) and higher taxes.
How much SIP needed for 1 crore?
At a 12% annual return, a monthly SIP of 10,000 takes about 20 years to reach 1 Crore. You can reach this goal much faster (around 15 years) by increasing your SIP amount by 10% every year using the Step-up feature.
Are SIP returns taxable in India?
Yes. For Equity SIPs, Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) over 1.25 Lakh are taxed at 12.5%. Short-term gains (held < 1 year) are taxed at 20%. Every installment is treated as a fresh investment for tax purposes.
Can I change my SIP date after starting?
Most Indian fund houses and apps (like Groww, Zerodha, or CAMS) allow you to modify the SIP date. If the "Modify" option isn't available, you can simply stop the current SIP and start a new one with your preferred date.
What is the best date for SIP in India?
Historically, there is no "perfect" date due to market volatility. However, choosing a date right after your salary credit (like 1st to 7th) is best to ensure investment discipline before you spend the money.
Does SIP guarantee returns?
No, SIPs in mutual funds do not guarantee returns as they are linked to the stock market. However, Indian market history shows that staying invested for 7-10 years significantly reduces risk and offers high inflation-adjusted growth.
Can I start SIP with only 500?
Yes, the majority of Mutual Fund schemes in India allow you to start an SIP with just 500 per month. Some AMC schemes even offer "Micro-SIPs" starting as low as 100 per month.
Is it better to invest in SIP or Lumpsum?
SIP is better for most investors as it averages the cost of investment and reduces risk in volatile markets. Lumpsum is only recommended when you have a large sum and the market is at a significant low or valuation.
What is the lock-in period for SIP?
Standard SIPs have no lock-in period. However, ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Schemes) have a 3-year lock-in period. Note that in ELSS, every SIP installment is locked for 3 years from its specific investment date.
Can I have multiple SIPs in the same fund?
Yes, you can start multiple SIPs of different amounts or on different dates within the same mutual fund scheme. This is often used by investors to track different financial goals (like Child Education and Retirement) separately.
