How is SIP Maturity Calculated?

M = P × [((1 + i)n - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

M: Maturity Amount | P: Monthly Investment

i: Periodic Rate of Interest | n: Total Months

Understanding Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

What is an SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Instead of a large one-time lumpsum investment, SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals.

"Time in the market is more important than timing the market."

Benefits of Investing via SIP

Financial Discipline

Regular habit of saving.

Power of Compounding

Wealth multiplies over time.

Cost Averaging

Lower average unit cost.

Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

What is the 15-15-15 rule in Mutual Fund SIP?
The 15-15-15 rule states that an SIP of ₹15,000 per month for 15 years at an expected return of 15% can help you accumulate ₹1 Crore. It highlights the power of compounding over long durations.
How are SIP returns taxed in India (2026)?
LTCG for Holding over 1 year is taxed at 12.5% for gains above ₹1.25 Lakh. STCG is taxed at 20%.
Can I start with just ₹500?
Yes, most Indian mutual funds allow you to start an SIP with as little as ₹500 per month.
What is a Step-up SIP?
It is a feature where you increase your monthly investment by a fixed percentage every year, usually aligned with salary increments.
Is SIP risk-free?
No, it is subject to market risks. However, long-term SIPs significantly mitigate volatility.
Can I stop SIP midway?
Yes, SIPs are flexible. You can pause or stop them without any penalty from the AMC.
Does SIP offer 80C benefits?
Only ELSS funds qualify for tax deductions up to ₹1.5 Lakh under Section 80C.
What is Rupee Cost Averaging?
It means you buy more units when market is low and fewer when market is high, averaging out the cost.
Is Lumpsum better than SIP?
SIP is better for disciplined investing; Lumpsum works best when markets are significantly undervalued.
Can I change SIP date?
Yes, you can modify the SIP date through your AMC portal or investment platform.
