How is SIP Maturity Calculated?

M = P × [((1 + i)n - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

M: Maturity Amount

P: SIP Amount (Monthly)

i: Periodic Rate of Interest (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)

n: Total Number of Installments

Understanding Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

What is an SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Instead of a large one-time lumpsum investment, SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, or weekly).

It follows the concept of "Time in the market is more important than timing the market."

Why choose Step-up SIP?

As your income grows every year, your savings should too. A Step-up SIP automatically increases your contribution by a fixed percentage or amount annually.

"Even a 10% annual increase in SIP can lead to a significantly larger corpus over 20 years compared to a flat SIP."

SIP Frequently Asked Questions (India 2026)

What is the 15-15-15 rule in Mutual Fund SIP?
The 15-15-15 rule states that an SIP of ₹15,000 per month for 15 years at an expected return of 15% can help you accumulate ₹1 Crore. It highlights the power of compounding over long durations.
How are SIP returns taxed in India (2026)?
For Equity Mutual Funds, Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) for holding periods over 1 year are taxed at 12.5% for gains exceeding ₹1.25 Lakh. Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) are taxed at 20%. Every installment is treated as a fresh investment for tax calculations.
Is SIP better than FD in India?
While FDs offer safety, Equity SIPs typically provide higher inflation-beating returns (12-15%) over 5+ years. SIPs are ideal for wealth creation, whereas FDs are better for short-term capital safety.
What happens if I miss my SIP payment?
If you miss a payment, the fund house does not charge a penalty, but your bank might charge for a "cheque bounce" or "ECS return." Missing three consecutive payments may result in the SIP being automatically cancelled by the AMC.
Can I withdraw SIP money anytime?
Yes, most open-ended SIPs allow withdrawal anytime. However, ELSS (Tax Saving) funds have a mandatory 3-year lock-in. For others, an exit load of 1% might apply if withdrawn within 1 year.
Which is the best date for SIP?
Historically, market timing via SIP dates doesn't significantly impact long-term returns. It is best to choose a date between the 1st and 7th of the month to ensure your investment is automated right after salary credit.
Can I stop SIP midway?
Yes, you can stop your SIP at any time without penalty. Your existing invested units will remain in the fund and continue to grow based on market performance until you choose to redeem them.
How much SIP is needed for ₹1 Crore?
To reach ₹1 Crore in 20 years at a 12% return, you need an SIP of approx. ₹10,000. By increasing your SIP by 10% annually (Step-up), you can reach this goal in just 15 years with the same initial amount.
Is SIP better than Lumpsum?
SIP is generally better for most investors as it averages the cost of purchase through market cycles. Lumpsum is better only if you have a large surplus and the market is significantly undervalued.
What is Rupee Cost Averaging?
It’s an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount regularly. This results in buying more units when the NAV is low and fewer when it's high, bringing down the average cost per unit over time.
What is Step-up SIP?
Step-up SIP is a feature where you increase your monthly investment by a fixed percentage (e.g., 10%) every year. This helps you reach goals faster as your annual income increases.
Is SIP risk-free?
No, SIPs in mutual funds are subject to market risks. However, the risk of loss reduces significantly over long tenures (7-10+ years) in equity funds due to the compounding effect and market cycles.
Can I change my SIP amount later?
Yes, you can modify your SIP amount at any time through your investment app. Alternatively, you can stop the existing SIP and start a new one with your desired higher or lower amount.
Does SIP provide Section 80C benefits?
Only SIPs in ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Schemes) qualify for tax deductions up to ₹1.5 Lakh under Section 80C. Other equity or debt SIPs do not offer this specific tax-saving benefit.
