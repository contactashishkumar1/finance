How is FD Maturity Calculated?

A = P × (1 + r/n)n×t

A: Maturity Amount | P: Principal Deposit Amount

r: Annual Interest Rate (Decimal)

n: Compounding frequency per year | t: Total Tenure (Years)

Example FD Calculation (India)

If you deposit ₹1,00,000 for 5 years at an interest rate of 7% p.a. compounded quarterly:
  • Principal Amount: ₹1,00,000
  • Total Interest Earned: ₹41,478
  • Maturity Value: ₹1,41,478

Impact of Compounding Frequency

Compounding frequency determines how often the interest is added back to your principal. The more frequent the compounding, the higher your final maturity value.

FD vs SIP – Which is Better?

Fixed Deposit (FD)

Safety & Fixed Returns: FD is ideal for conservative investors who prioritize capital protection. You get a guaranteed return at a fixed interest rate, making it safe from market fluctuations.

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

Market Linked & Growth: SIPs in mutual funds are subject to market risks but offer potentially higher long-term returns. They are better for long-term goals (5-10+ years) where inflation-beating growth is required.

FD Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. What is the difference between Cumulative and Non-cumulative FD?
In a Cumulative FD, interest is reinvested and paid at maturity (compounded). In a Non-cumulative FD, interest is paid out at regular intervals (monthly or quarterly), providing a regular income stream.
2. Is interest earned on FD taxable?
Yes, FD interest is taxable as per your income tax slab. Banks also deduct TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) if the interest earned across all your FDs in a bank exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens) in a financial year.
3. What is a Tax Saving FD?
A Tax Saving FD has a mandatory lock-in period of 5 years. Investments up to ₹1.5 Lakh are eligible for deduction under Section 80C. Note that the interest earned on this FD is still taxable.
4. Can I withdraw my FD before maturity?
Yes, most banks allow premature withdrawal, but they usually charge a penalty (typically 0.5% to 1% reduction in the interest rate applicable for the period the FD was held).
5. Do senior citizens get higher FD rates?
Yes, most Indian banks offer an additional 0.50% interest rate to senior citizens (individuals aged 60 and above) on their Fixed Deposits.
6. What is the DICGC insurance on FDs?
As per RBI guidelines, the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) insures your deposits (including principal and interest) up to a maximum of ₹5 Lakh per bank.
7. Can a minor open an FD account in India?
Yes, Fixed Deposits can be opened in the name of a minor. The account must be operated by a natural or legal guardian until the minor attains the age of 18 years.
8. What is the minimum and maximum amount for an FD?
The minimum amount varies by bank but is often as low as ₹1,000 or ₹5,000. There is generally no theoretical maximum limit, though deposits above ₹2 Crore are often categorized as 'Bulk Deposits' with different rates.
9. What is a Flexi Fixed Deposit?
A Flexi FD is linked to your savings or current account. It automatically transfers surplus funds into an FD to earn higher interest, while allowing you to withdraw money as needed.
10. Can I get a loan against my Fixed Deposit?
Yes, most banks offer a loan or overdraft facility against FDs. You can typically borrow up to 90-95% of the FD value at a rate usually 1-2% higher than the FD interest rate.
11. What happens if I don't provide my PAN details?
If you do not provide your PAN, TDS will be deducted at a higher rate of 20% instead of the standard 10%. Additionally, you will not be able to claim credit for the tax deducted.
12. What is an NRE Fixed Deposit?
Non-Resident External (NRE) FDs are for NRIs to invest foreign earnings in INR. A key benefit is that the interest earned on NRE FDs is completely tax-free in India.
13. Does compounding frequency affect my final returns?
Yes. The more frequently interest is compounded, the higher the effective yield. Monthly compounding results in a slightly higher maturity amount than quarterly or yearly compounding.
14. Can I add a nominee to my FD account?
Yes, adding a nominee is highly recommended. In the event of the depositor's death, the nominee can claim the maturity amount easily without extensive legal documentation.

Quick Summary

• Fixed Deposits (FD) offer safe and guaranteed returns for a fixed tenure.

• Compounding frequency (Monthly/Quarterly) impacts your total interest earned.

• Senior citizens usually enjoy 0.5% higher interest rates than general citizens.
