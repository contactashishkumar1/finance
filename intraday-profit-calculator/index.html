What is Intraday Trading?

Intraday trading is the practice of buying and selling financial instruments (like stocks) within the same trading day. The primary goal is to capitalize on short-term price movements. In India, most intraday traders use margin (leverage) provided by their brokers to increase their exposure.

However, intraday trading is not just about the stock price move. Because the number of trades is high, statutory costs like STT and GST can significantly impact your bottom line. Professional traders always use an Intraday Profit Calculator to determine their break-even point before placing an order.

How Intraday Profit is Calculated

Calculating the true profit of a day trade requires a two-step process: finding the Gross P&L and then subtracting the complex statutory charges.

1. Gross Profit/Loss

This is the simple difference between your selling price and buying price, multiplied by the quantity. If you buy at ₹1000 and sell at ₹1005 with 100 shares, your Gross Profit is ₹500.

2. Net Take-Home Profit

This is the amount that actually hits your ledger. It is Gross Profit − (Brokerage + STT + Transaction Charges + GST + SEBI Fees + Stamp Duty).

Why Brokerage and Taxes Matter

Many beginners fail because they chase "small profits" that are completely wiped out by charges. For example, a ₹0.50 move in a ₹1000 stock might look like a profit, but once you account for the STT (0.025% on sell) and the ₹40 flat brokerage (buy + sell), you might actually be in a net loss. This is known as the "Negative Yield" trap.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is intraday profit?
Intraday profit is the realized gain from buying and selling a stock on the same day, after deducting all trading costs like brokerage and taxes.
How much are the charges for intraday trading in India?
Charges include Brokerage (approx ₹20), STT (0.025% on sell), Transaction fees (0.00297%), GST (18% on fees), and SEBI charges. Use the calculator above for an exact breakdown.
Is intraday trading profitable?
Intraday trading can be profitable for disciplined traders with a strong risk management plan. However, a high win rate is required to overcome the impact of recurring transaction costs.
Why does brokerage matter so much?
Because it is a fixed cost. On a small trade, a ₹40 brokerage could be 50% of your gross profit. Professional traders use discount brokers to keep this cost capped.
