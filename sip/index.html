How is SIP Maturity Calculated?

M = P × [((1 + i)n - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

M: Maturity Amount | P: Monthly Investment

i: Periodic Rate of Interest (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)

n: Total Number of Installments (Months)

SIP Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. What is the 15-15-15 rule in Mutual Fund SIP?
The 15-15-15 rule states that an SIP of ₹15,000 per month for 15 years at an expected return of 15% can help you accumulate ₹1 Crore. It showcases the power of long-term compounding.
2. How are SIP returns taxed in India (2026)?
For Equity Mutual Funds, LTCG on holding periods over 1 year are taxed at 12.5% for gains above ₹1.25 Lakh. STCG is taxed at 20%. Note that every SIP installment is treated as a new investment for tax purposes. Use our EMI Calculator for better debt-asset balancing.
3. Is SIP better than FD in India?
Historically, Equity SIPs have offered 12-15% returns, beating FD rates which hover around 6-7%. While FDs offer capital safety, SIPs are essential for long-term wealth creation.
4. Can I withdraw SIP money anytime?
Yes, except for ELSS funds (3-year lock-in), you can withdraw anytime. Exit loads of ~1% may apply if you withdraw within one year.
5. What happens if I miss my SIP payment?
Missing a few payments doesn't attract AMC penalties, but your bank might charge for an ECS return. After three misses, the SIP is often cancelled.
6. Which is the best date for SIP in India?
Historically, market timing doesn't matter for 10-year SIPs. Choose a date between the 1st and 7th (post-salary) to ensure discipline.
7. Can I stop my SIP midway?
Yes, you can stop SIP at any time. Your existing units will grow based on market NAV until you redeem them.
8. How much SIP is needed for ₹1 Crore?
At 12% returns, you need approx. ₹10,500 monthly for 20 years. Using Step-up (10%/year), you can reach it in 15 years.
9. Is SIP better than Lumpsum?
SIP reduces market timing risk through Rupee Cost Averaging. Lumpsum is better only when markets are massive undervalued.
10. What is Step-up SIP?
Step-up SIP increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage annually. This aligns with salary growth and reaches goals faster.
11. Is Section 80C applicable to SIP?
Only ELSS funds qualify for Section 80C deductions up to ₹1.5 Lakh. Other equity SIPs do not.
12. Can I change my SIP amount?
Yes, most apps allow "Edit SIP". Alternatively, stop the existing one and start a new one with the desired amount.
13. Does SIP guarantee returns?
No, it is subject to market risks. However, historic data shows that 7-10+ year SIPs significantly reduce risk.
14. Is SIP risk-free for retirees?
Retirees should prefer Debt or Hybrid SIPs for capital preservation while allowing some growth.
