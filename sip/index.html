How is SIP Maturity Calculated?

M = P × [((1 + i)n - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

M: Maturity Amount | P: Monthly Investment

i: Periodic Rate of Interest (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)

n: Total Number of Installments (Months)

SIP vs Lumpsum – Which is Better?

Deciding between a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) and a Lumpsum investment depends on your cash flow and market outlook. While Lumpsum allows your entire capital to compound from day one, it exposes you to market timing risk.

  • SIP: Best for consistent earners. It uses "Rupee Cost Averaging" to lower the cost of units during market dips. Check your weighted costs with our Stock Average Tool.
  • Lumpsum: Potentially higher returns if invested during a market correction. The entire amount compounds immediately.

What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount of money in mutual funds at regular intervals (usually monthly). Instead of making a large one-time investment, you spread it out over time, allowing for Rupee Cost Averaging.

When the market is low, your monthly amount buys more units. When the market is high, it buys fewer units. Over time, this lowers your average cost per unit and helps you stay disciplined regardless of market volatility.

SIP vs Fixed Deposit (FD) – What’s Better?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 15-15-15 rule in Mutual Fund SIP?
The 15-15-15 rule states that an SIP of ₹15,000 per month for 15 years at an expected return of 15% can help you accumulate ₹1 Crore. It is a simplified goal-setting rule for Indian investors.
How are SIP returns taxed in India (2026)?
For Equity Mutual Funds, LTCG on holding periods over 1 year are taxed at 12.5% for gains above ₹1.25 Lakh. STCG is taxed at 20%. Note that every SIP installment is treated as a new investment for tax purposes. Plan your exits by calculating Income Tax liability accurately.
Is SIP better than FD in India?
Historically, Equity SIPs have offered 12-15% returns, beating Fixed Deposit rates which hover around 6-7%. While FDs offer capital safety, SIPs are essential for long-term wealth creation.
Can I withdraw SIP money anytime?
Yes, except for ELSS funds (3-year lock-in), you can withdraw anytime. Exit loads of ~1% may apply if you withdraw within one year. Use your SIP to build a liquid asset base for your Net Worth.
Does SIP guarantee returns?
No, it is subject to market risks. However, historic data shows that 7-10+ year SIPs significantly reduce risk and outpace Inflation.
What is Step-up SIP?
Step-up SIP increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage annually. This aligns with salary growth and reaches your ₹1 Crore goal much faster.
