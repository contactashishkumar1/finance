How is Future Value Calculated?

FV = PV × (1 + r/n)nt

FV: Future Value | PV: Present Value (Initial Investment)

r: Annual Interest Rate (as decimal)

n: Compounding frequency (1 for annual, 12 for monthly)

t: Time period in years

Example FV Calculation (India)

If you invest ₹1,00,000 for 10 years at an expected return of 12% p.a. (compounded annually):

  • Future Value: ₹3,10,585
  • Total Wealth Gained: ₹2,10,585

What is Future Value (FV)?

Future Value (FV) is a financial concept that determines the value of a current asset at a specified date in the future, based on an assumed rate of growth. In India, investors use FV to plan for long-term goals like retirement planning, child education, or buying property. Understanding FV helps you see how inflation-adjusted returns or high-growth equity investments can multiply your wealth.

Retirement Fund 20+ Years Exponential Wealth

Future Value vs CAGR – What's the Difference?

While Fixed Deposits (FD) have a fixed FV, equity investments are often measured by CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate). FV tells you the final amount you will receive, whereas CAGR tells you the annual rate at which your money grew. Both are critical for lumpsum investment planning.

Future Value FAQs (2026)

1. What is Future Value (FV)?
Future Value is the estimated worth of a current asset at a future date based on an assumed rate of growth and compounding.
2. How is Future Value calculated?
FV is calculated using the formula FV = PV × (1 + r/n)^(nt), where PV is present value, r is rate, n is compounding frequency, and t is time.
3. Does compounding frequency affect the final amount?
Yes, the more frequently interest is compounded (e.g., monthly), the higher the Future Value due to interest being earned on interest more often.
4. What is the difference between FV and CAGR?
FV is the total amount you will have in the future, while CAGR is the annual growth rate that gets you there.
5. Is inflation considered in Future Value?
Standard FV calculations don't account for inflation. You should subtract the inflation rate from your expected return to see the "Real" Future Value.
