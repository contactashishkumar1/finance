What is Future Value (FV)?

Future Value (FV) is a financial concept that determines the value of a current asset at a specific date in the future based on an assumed rate of growth. It is the cornerstone of compound interest calculations, helping investors understand how much their current money will be worth after a period of time.

Future Value Formula

FV = PV × (1 + r)^n

PV: Present Value (Current Investment)

r: Expected rate of interest per period

n: Number of compounding periods

Example Calculation

Suppose you invest ₹1,00,000 for 10 years at an expected return of 10%.

  • PV = ₹1,00,000
  • r = 0.10 (10%)
  • n = 10
  • FV = 1,00,000 × (1.10)^10 = ₹2,59,374

Future Value vs CAGR

While Future Value tells you the absolute amount your investment will reach, CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) tells you the speed at which your money grew over that period. FV is a projection of the end result, whereas CAGR is the smoothed annual rate of return.

Common Questions

How is Future Value calculated? ▼
Future Value is calculated using the compound interest formula. It takes your initial principal and adds the interest earned in each period back to the principal for the next period's calculation.
Does FV guarantee returns? ▼
No, FV is an estimate based on assumptions. Actual returns depend on market conditions, the type of instrument (Equity vs. Debt), and interest rate fluctuations over the investment period.
Does inflation affect Future Value? ▼
Yes. While FV shows you the nominal amount, its purchasing power will be lower due to inflation. To calculate "Real FV," you should use an inflation-adjusted interest rate.
