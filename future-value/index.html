What is Future Value (FV)?

Future Value (FV) is a financial estimation tool used to calculate the value of a current asset at a specified date in the future, based on an assumed rate of growth. It determines how much your current investment will be worth after a certain number of years, accounting for the power of compound interest.

For Indian investors, calculating the Future Value is essential for long-term goal planning. Whether you are investing in a Fixed Deposit (FD), Public Provident Fund (PPF), or look at mutual fund projections through an SIP Calculator, knowing the end maturity value helps in deciding capital allocation.

Future Value Formula

FV = PV × (1 + r)^n

PV: Present Value (Current Amount)

r: Rate of interest per period

n: Number of compounding periods

Why Future Value Matters

Understanding Future Value allows you to plan for specific life milestones like buying a home, children's education, or retirement. It helps investors visualize the opportunity cost of spending money today versus investing it for the future.

When compared with our Inflation Calculator, Future Value helps you understand if your current maturity projection will be sufficient to meet the rising costs of living 20 years from now.

Future Value vs CAGR

While Future Value determines the final amount your investment will grow into, CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) measures the annual rate of return you need to reach that goal. You can use our CAGR Calculator to analyze past performance, while the FV tool is used to predict future wealth based on expected returns.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Future Value calculated in India? ▼
Future Value is calculated using the compound interest formula. It takes your initial principal and adds the interest earned in each period back to the balance, which then earns more interest.
Does FV guarantee returns? ▼
No, Future Value is a mathematical estimate based on assumptions. Actual returns on market-linked investments like mutual funds or stocks may vary.
Does inflation affect the Future Value calculation? ▼
Standard FV formulas do not account for inflation. To find the "real" value, you should use an inflation-adjusted interest rate (Expected Return - Predicted Inflation).
What interest rate should I assume for FV? ▼
For fixed income like PPF, use current govt rates (~7%). For long-term equity mutual funds, an assumption of 10-12% is generally considered standard for projections.
