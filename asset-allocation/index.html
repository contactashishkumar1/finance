How is Asset Allocation Calculated?

Equity % = (100 - Your Age) ± Risk Adjustment %

Base Formula: The standard "100 minus age" rule determines the equity floor.

Risk Adjustment: +10% for Aggressive, 0% for Moderate, -10% for Conservative profiles.

Remainder: Allocated to Debt (80%) and Gold/Cash (20%) for a balanced Net Worth strategy.

Example Analysis (India)

If you are 30 years old with a Moderate risk profile:
  • Base Equity: 100 - 30 = 70%
  • Debt Component: (100 - 70) * 0.8 = 24%
  • Gold/Cash: (100 - 70) * 0.2 = 6%

The Importance of Asset Allocation in 2026

Asset allocation is often described as the only "free lunch" in investing. It is the strategy of balancing risk and reward by apportioning a portfolio's assets according to an individual's goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. In the volatile Indian markets of 2026, relying on a single asset class—whether it's just stocks or just fixed deposits—is a recipe for financial instability.

Our Asset Allocation Calculator uses your age as the primary anchor for risk. This is based on the biological reality that younger investors have more time to recover from market downturns and can therefore afford higher equity exposure via Monthly SIPs. As you age, the focus of your portfolio naturally shifts from wealth accumulation to capital preservation.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the 100 minus age rule?
The 100 minus age rule is a simple thumb rule for asset allocation where you subtract your age from 100 to find the percentage of your portfolio that should be in equities.
2. Why is asset allocation important?
Asset allocation is the most important factor in determining portfolio risk. It ensures you aren't over-exposed to a single asset class like stocks during a market crash.
3. Does asset allocation change if I have more money?
Technically, no. Percentages stay the same based on risk appetite, but larger portfolios might include more alternative assets like real estate.
4. How often should I update my asset allocation?
It is recommended to review and rebalance your portfolio once a year or after major life events to keep it aligned with your goals.
5. Is 100% equity allocation ever a good idea?
Only for very long-term goals (15+ years) and high risk tolerance. Even then, most advisors suggest a small portion in debt for liquidity.
6. Should I count my primary home in asset allocation?
Typically, no. Your primary residence is a lifestyle asset. Only investment properties (real estate) should be counted in your investable asset mix.
7. What is the role of Gold in allocation?
Gold acts as a hedge against inflation and currency depreciation. A 5-10% allocation is standard for portfolio stability.
8. How does my retirement age affect this?
The closer you get to retirement, the more your allocation should shift from equity (growth) to debt (capital preservation).
