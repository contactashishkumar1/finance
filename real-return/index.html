Real Return Formula

Real Return = ((1 + Nominal Return) / (1 + Inflation)) − 1

This formula provides the exact rate of purchasing power growth annually. It is the cornerstone of Financial Independence planning.

Real Return vs. Purchasing Power

While the Real Rate of Return is a percentage, Purchasing Power is the practical application of that percentage. Inflation is often called the "Silent Tax" because it reduces what your money can buy without a single rupee ever leaving your bank account.

If the cost of a standard basket of goods in India (milk, fuel, rent) rises by 6% annually, your income or investments must grow by at least 6.1% to maintain your current lifestyle. Anything less results in a "Standard of Living" decline. Use our Inflation Tool to see how specific costs like healthcare or education rise even faster.

How Taxes Impact Your Real Rate of Return

For a realistic assessment, you must consider the Post-Tax Real Return. Taxes and inflation work together to erode wealth. For example, if you are in the 30% tax bracket:

  • Investment Return (Nominal): 7.00%
  • Tax Paid (30% of 7%): - 2.10%
  • Post-Tax Return: 4.90%
  • Inflation Rate: - 6.00%
  • Actual Real Return: - 1.10%

In this common scenario, you are actually losing money in real terms every year. To stay positive, you need higher-alpha investments. Check your potential Income Tax slabs to see your exact deduction levels.

What is Real Return?

Real return is the actual profit or loss you make on an investment after adjusting for the effects of Inflation. While nominal returns tell you the "face value" growth of your money, real returns tell you the actual growth in your purchasing power. This is why aggressive savers often use a FIRE Calculator to determine their exit number based on real terms.

Whether you invest via a monthly SIP or a one-time lumpsum, the principle remains the same: if your return doesn't beat inflation, you are effectively losing wealth. Use this tool alongside an XIRR Calculator to find your true portfolio performance.

Frequently Asked Questions (Real Return)

1. What is the Real Rate of Return?
The real rate of return is the net profit or loss you earn on an investment after adjusting for inflation. It represents the increase or decrease in your actual purchasing power over time.
2. Real Return vs Nominal Return: What is the difference?
Nominal return is the literal percentage gain (e.g., 10%). Real return accounts for the fact that prices for goods also rose (e.g., 6% inflation). Your real growth is roughly the difference between the two.
3. How is inflation-adjusted return calculated?
The precise formula is: ((1 + Nominal Rate) / (1 + Inflation Rate)) - 1. For example, if you earn 12% and inflation is 6%, the real return is roughly 5.66%, not just 6%.
4. Can real return be negative?
Yes. If inflation is higher than your investment return, your real return is negative. This is common in savings accounts or short-term deposits when inflation spikes.
