What is a Car Loan Calculator?

A Car Loan Calculator (or auto loan calculator) is a specialized financial tool designed to help car buyers determine their monthly liability before making a purchase. By providing the car price and down payment, it allows you to see the actual amount you need to borrow and how your interest rates impact your monthly cash flow.

In India, car loans are typically offered for tenures of 3 to 7 years. Use this tool to find a balance between a comfortable EMI and the total interest cost. Before finalising your car, you may also want to check your Loan Eligibility based on your monthly income.

How Car Loan EMI is Calculated

Car loans in India generally use the Reducing Balance Method. Our calculator uses the standard mathematical formula for amortized loans:

EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]
  • P (Principal): The actual loan amount (Price - Down Payment).
  • r (Monthly Interest Rate): Yearly rate divided by 12 and then by 100.
  • n (Number of Months): Total tenure years multiplied by 12.

Factors Affecting Your Car Loan EMI

Interest Rate

Unlike home loans, car loan rates are usually fixed. Even a 1% difference can change your EMI significantly.

Loan Tenure

Most car loans are for 5 years. Extending to 7 years reduces EMI but increases the total interest burden.

Down Payment

Higher down payments reduce your principal amount, which lowers your interest and EMI instantly.

Example Car Loan Calculation

Suppose you are buying a car worth ₹10,00,000. You pay ₹2,00,000 as down payment, leaving a loan amount of ₹8,00,000. At a 9% interest rate for 5 years:

  • Monthly EMI: ₹16,607
  • Total Interest: ₹1,96,403
  • Total Repayment: ₹9,96,403

Using a Loan Prepayment strategy can help you save a large portion of that interest if you have surplus cash later.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical interest rate for car loans in India?
In 2026, car loan rates in India generally range from 8.5% to 12%, depending on the bank, your credit score, and whether the car is new or used.
How much down payment is required?
Most banks require a minimum down payment of 10-20% of the on-road price. Some premium salary accounts may offer 100% on-road funding.
Is a longer tenure better for a car loan?
A longer tenure (7 years) makes the EMI affordable but you end up paying significantly more interest. Since cars are depreciating assets, a 3-5 year tenure is usually recommended.
Can I use my SIP savings to pay for a car?
Yes. If you have been investing in a SIP for 3-5 years, using that corpus for a higher down payment can save you lakhs in loan interest.
What documents are required for a car loan?
Typically: ID proof, Address proof, Salary slips (3 months), ITR (2 years), and Bank statements (6 months).
