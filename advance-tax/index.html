Who Should Pay Advance Tax?

Advance tax is mandatory for all taxpayers in India whose estimated tax liability for the financial year is ₹10,000 or more after adjusting for TDS and TCS.

  • Salaried Individuals: Generally, your employer deducts TDS from your salary. However, if you have significant income from rentals, fixed deposit interest, or Capital Gains, you might still need to pay advance tax.
  • Business Owners & Professionals: Freelancers and businesses must calculate their expected profits and pay quarterly installments to remain compliant.
  • NRIs: Non-resident Indians earning income in India (like house property rent or stock profits) are also liable if the ₹10,000 threshold is met.

How to Avoid Advance Tax Penalty (Section 234C)

Section 234C interest is charged at 1% per month if you fail to pay the required percentage of tax by the quarterly due dates. Here is how to plan your payments:

  • Stick to the Schedule: Pay 15% by June, 45% by September, 75% by December, and 100% by March 15th.
  • Estimate Conservatively: It is better to over-estimate your income slightly than to underpay and attract interest under Section 234B and 234C.
  • Re-calculate Quarterly: As your Investment returns fluctuate, revisit this calculator to adjust your next installment.

Advance Tax Laws in India

Advance tax is the income tax that must be paid "as you earn" throughout the financial year. Any taxpayer whose estimated tax liability (after TDS) exceeds ₹10,000 for the year is legally obligated to pay advance tax. This ensures that the government maintains a steady cash flow and reduces the financial burden on taxpayers at the end of the year.

Failing to pay on time results in interest penalties under Section 234C (for quarterly delay) and Section 234B (for overall shortfall at year-end). Plan your exits using tax-loss harvesting to lower this liability legally.

Advance Tax FAQs

1. What are the due dates for Advance Tax payments?
Installments are due on June 15 (15%), Sept 15 (45%), Dec 15 (75%), and March 15 (100%). For professionals under Section 44ADA, 100% is due by March 15.
2. Are senior citizens exempt from Advance Tax?
Yes, resident individuals aged 60 years or older who do not have any income from "Profits and Gains of Business or Profession" are exempt from paying Advance Tax.
3. What happens if I pay more Advance Tax than required?
If your Advance Tax payments exceed your final liability, you can claim a refund while filing your Income Tax Return (ITR). You may also earn interest on the refund amount.
4. How is Section 234C interest calculated?
Interest under Section 234C is charged at 1% per month for three months on the amount of shortfall in each installment. For the last installment in March, interest is charged for one month.
5. Is Advance Tax mandatory for NRIs?
Yes. If an NRI has income in India (like rental income, interest, or capital gains) and the tax liability after TDS is more than ₹10,000, they must pay Advance Tax.
