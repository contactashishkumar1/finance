Reward Calculation Logic

Net Value = (Annual Spend × Rate × Pt. Value) + Milestones - (Fee × 1.18)

Point Valuation: Reward rates are multiplied by your custom point value to find the real rupee impact.

Fee Waiver: If your total annual spend hits the bank's target, the annual fee (and its 18% GST) is excluded from costs.

Effective Rate: Calculated as Net Benefit ÷ Total Spend. A rate above 2% is generally considered excellent in India.

Credit Card Rewards in 2026: The Reality Check

In the current Indian banking landscape, credit cards are no longer just for credit; they are financial optimization tools. However, with banks frequenting "devaluations"—reducing reward rates and increasing fees—it is vital to audit your wallet every year.

A "premium" card with a ₹5,000 fee might be costing you money if your monthly spend doesn't hit the required milestones. Conversely, a co-branded card (like ICICI Amazon Pay or Axis Flipkart) might offer higher value for online shoppers even with a lower base rate. For high-frequency travelers, the value of 1 Reward Point can range from ₹0.20 to ₹1.00 depending on the redemption partner. Track your total portfolio growth with our Net Worth Calculator.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it worth paying a ₹5,000 annual fee?
It depends on your "Effective Reward Rate." If the card provides benefits like Unlimited Lounge access, Concierge services, and Milestone vouchers worth ₹15,000, then the ₹5,000 fee is a profitable investment. Use this calculator to see if your Net Benefit is positive.
How to calculate the value of 1 Reward Point?
Divide the Rupee value of the reward item by the points required to get it. For example, if a ₹500 voucher requires 2,000 points, each point is worth ₹0.25. Always check redemption ratios for Air Miles or Vouchers before calculating.
Does GST apply to credit card fees?
Yes, in India, an 18% Goods and Services Tax (GST) is levied on all credit card fees, including joining fees, annual renewal fees, and interest charges. Our calculator accounts for this extra 18% cost automatically unless the fee is waived.
