What is a Personal Loan EMI?

A Personal Loan EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed monthly payment that you make to a lender to repay an unsecured loan. Unlike a Home Loan, a personal loan doesn't require collateral, which is why the interest rates are generally higher, typically ranging between 10.5% and 24% p.a. in India.

The EMI stays constant throughout the tenure, helping you plan your monthly budget. In the early stages of the loan, a larger part of your EMI goes toward paying off the interest. Over time, as the principal reduces, more of the payment goes toward clearing the balance. Use our Loan Eligibility Tool to see if you qualify for a specific amount based on your salary.

How Personal Loan EMI is Calculated

Financial institutions use an amortized reducing balance formula to calculate EMIs. The mathematical logic is as follows:

EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]
  • P: Principal Loan Amount.
  • r: Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100).
  • n: Loan Tenure in Months (Years × 12).

Example: Loan of ₹5 Lakh

Consider a personal loan scenario popular among Indian salaried professionals:

Loan Amount: ₹5,00,000

Interest Rate: 12%

Tenure: 5 Years

Monthly EMI: ₹11,122

Total Interest: ₹1,67,333

By the end of the 5 years, you would have repaid nearly 33% extra in interest. This is why aggressive Debt Payoff strategies are essential for unsecured loans.

Tips to Reduce Your Personal Loan Burden

Shorter Tenure

While it increases monthly EMI, it drastically cuts the total interest payout. Aim for 3 years instead of 5 if possible.

Early Prepayment

Any extra payment directly reduces the principal. In India, check if your bank charges a foreclosure penalty before prepaying.

Balance Transfer

If your CIBIL score has improved, switch to a lender offering a 2-3% lower rate to reduce your long-term cost.

Investment Spread

Ensure your Portfolio CAGR is higher than the loan rate; otherwise, pay off the debt first.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between personal and home loans?
Personal loans are unsecured (no collateral) and have higher interest rates. Home loans are secured by the property and offer much lower rates along with tax benefits.
What is a good credit score for a personal loan?
A CIBIL score of 750 or above is considered ideal. It helps you get faster approvals and the lowest possible interest rates from Indian banks.
Does prepayment help in personal loans?
Yes. Since interest is high (12-18%), any extra payment toward the principal reduces your interest burden significantly. Always check for a 'Lock-in' period or foreclosure charges.
