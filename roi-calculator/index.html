What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment. It measures the amount of return on an investment, relative to the investment’s cost. To calculate ROI, the benefit (or return) of an investment is divided by the cost of the investment. The result is expressed as a percentage or a ratio.

For Indian investors, ROI is the most common metric used to judge a Mutual Fund SIP or a stock market trade. Unlike our CAGR Calculator, which provides the annualized growth, ROI provides the simple absolute growth of the capital invested.

ROI vs. CAGR: What's the Difference?

While both metrics measure returns, they serve different purposes:

  • ROI (Simple Return): Tells you exactly how much money you made in total. It does not factor in "time." If you double your money in 2 years or 10 years, your ROI is still 100%.
  • CAGR (Time-Adjusted Return): Tells you how fast your money grew on an annual basis. Doubling money in 2 years is roughly 41% CAGR, while doing it in 10 years is only 7.2% CAGR.

How Businesses Use ROI

In the business world, ROI is used to evaluate capital expenditures (CapEx) or marketing campaigns. A business might invest ₹5 Lakhs into a new piece of equipment and hope for an ROI of 20% per year to justify the purchase. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ROI for an investment?
In India, a long-term ROI that beats inflation (approx 6%) and Fixed Deposit rates (approx 7%) is considered good. For equity investments, a 12-15% annual return is generally the target.
How do I calculate ROI?
Subtract the initial cost from the final value to find your profit. Divide that profit by the initial cost and multiply by 100 to get the percentage.
Is ROI better than CAGR?
ROI is simpler for quick snapshots, but CAGR is much better for comparing investments over different durations because it accounts for the time value of money.
Can ROI be negative?
Yes. If your final value is less than your initial investment, your ROI will be a negative percentage, representing a loss.
