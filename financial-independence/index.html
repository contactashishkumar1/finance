How is the FI Number Calculated?

FI Corpus = (Future Annual Expenses) / Safe Withdrawal Rate

Future Expenses: Current expenses compounded by inflation until the FI year.

Withdrawal Rate: Usually 4% (25X Rule) or lower for higher safety. Check withdrawal impact here.

Corpus: The amount needed to generate that income indefinitely. Track your progress with a Net Worth tracker.

What is Financial Independence (FIRE)?

Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE) is a movement dedicated to a program of extreme savings and investment that allows proponents to retire far earlier than traditional budgets and retirement plans would allow. Achieving financial independence means your "Passive Income"—earned through investments, rentals, or business—exceeds your monthly living expenses.

In the Indian context of 2026, achieving FIRE requires a nuanced understanding of **Cost of Living** and **Inflation**. Unlike Western countries, India faces higher inflation (typically 5-7% p.a.), which means your future expenses will be significantly higher than they are today. A successful FI plan must account for this "inflation drag" using tools like a SIP Calculator or Lumpsum Tool.

Types of FIRE Strategies

There are several ways to approach your financial freedom goal, depending on your lifestyle preferences:

  • Lean FIRE: Living on a minimalistic budget. This allows you to retire much earlier but with a frugal lifestyle.
  • Fat FIRE: Aiming for a luxury lifestyle post-retirement. This requires a larger corpus and often a longer career.
  • Barista FIRE: Quitting high-stress jobs and taking up part-time work to cover small gaps while your SWP plan provides the bulk of income.
  • Coast FIRE: Having enough in your accounts today that, without another cent invested, your portfolio will grow to your FI number by standard retirement age. Check your CAGR to track this growth.

FIRE Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is 1 Crore enough for financial independence in India?
For a family in a metro like Bangalore or Mumbai in 2026, ₹1 Crore only generates about ₹33,000 monthly (at 4% SWR), which is usually insufficient. Aim for higher targets using SIP planning.
2. How does the "Safe Withdrawal Rate" (SWR) work?
SWR is the percentage you withdraw every year. While 4% is common, 3% is better for early retirement to handle India's higher inflation rates.
3. Does FI include my house?
No. Your home is not a liquid asset. However, a paid-off home reduces your monthly expenses, effectively lowering your FI corpus requirement.
4. What is the biggest risk to financial independence?
Medical inflation and market sequence risk. Ensure you have high health insurance coverage separate from your FI fund.
5. Should I stop my SIP once I achieve FI?
Yes, you stop saving and start withdrawing. You transition from accumulation to distribution using a Systematic Withdrawal Plan.
6. How to invest for FIRE in India?
Use a mix of Index Funds, Flexicap funds, and debt for stability. The goal is to maximize post-tax real returns.
7. Does FIRE mean I will never work again?
No. It means you work because you want to. Many "retired" people start niche businesses or consult in their field of expertise.
8. Can I achieve FI with children?
Yes, but you must treat their milestones (Education, Marriage) as separate sinking funds outside your main FI corpus.
