What is No Cost EMI?

No Cost EMI is a payment option where you pay for a product in equal monthly installments without any additional interest charges. For example, if you buy a laptop worth ₹60,000 for 6 months, you simply pay ₹10,000 per month.

However, No Cost EMI is not truly "zero interest." RBI rules prohibit zero-interest loans. Instead, the merchant (like Amazon or Flipkart) gives you an upfront discount equal to the interest the bank will charge. Your monthly payment remains fixed, making it "feel" like interest-free credit.

How No Cost EMI Actually Works

Example The ₹50,000 Smartphone Purchase

  • Original Price: ₹50,000
  • Bank Interest (15% for 12 months): ~₹4,130
  • Merchant Discount: The store gives you a ₹4,130 discount instantly.
  • Loan Amount: Bank finances only ₹45,870.
  • Monthly EMI: You pay ₹50,000 / 12 = ₹4,166 per month.

Result: By the end of 12 months, you paid exactly ₹50,000. The interest was effectively paid by the discount provided by the store.

Hidden Cost in No Cost EMI

While the interest is offset, there are three common hidden costs you must factor in:

Processing Fees

Most banks charge a one-time non-refundable fee (e.g., ₹199) to initiate an EMI plan on your credit or debit card.

GST on Interest

The bank charges 18% GST on the monthly interest component. This GST is not covered by the merchant discount.

Lost Discounts

Sometimes, merchants offer an even lower price for cash/full-upfront payments. By choosing EMI, you may lose that extra discount.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is No Cost EMI really free?
It is free of "Interest" because the store gives a discount. However, you pay a processing fee and 18% GST on the interest component.
Is No Cost EMI available on debit cards?
Yes, most major banks like HDFC, ICICI, and SBI offer No Cost EMI on pre-approved debit cards for eligible customers.
Does it affect my credit score?
Yes. Since it is a loan, timely payments will improve your CIBIL score, while late payments will negatively impact it.
