How is Inventory Turnover Calculated?

Ratio = Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

COGS: Total direct costs of producing/buying goods sold.

Avg. Inventory: (Opening Stock + Closing Stock) / 2.

DSI: 365 / Inventory Turnover Ratio (Time to empty warehouse).

Maximizing Business Efficiency in 2026

In the competitive Indian business landscape of 2026, where e-commerce and quick-commerce (like Blinkit or Zepto) are setting new standards for speed, managing your **Inventory Turnover** is more critical than ever. The inventory turnover ratio is a key performance indicator (KPI) that tells you how efficiently your capital is working.

Money sitting in a warehouse as unsold stock is "dead capital." It prevents you from investing in new products, marketing, or expansion. The Inventory Turnover Calculator helps you identify if you are overstocked (low ratio) or if you are running too lean and risking "out-of-stock" situations (very high ratio).

What are Days Sales of Inventory (DSI)?

While the ratio tells you how many times you restock per year, DSI translates that into human-readable time. If your DSI is 90 days, it means every product you buy sits on your shelf for three months before being sold. In 2026, high-performing retail businesses aim for a DSI of 30-45 days, while perishable sectors like food and fashion strive for 15-20 days.

Inventory Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why use COGS instead of Sales in the formula?
Sales include a markup/profit, whereas inventory is recorded at cost. Using Sales would artificially inflate the ratio. COGS provides an "apple-to-apple" comparison with inventory costs.
2. What causes a very low turnover ratio?
Common causes include poor sales performance, over-ordering stock, seasonal demand drops, or holding "obsolete" stock that is no longer in demand but still on the books.
3. Can a turnover ratio be too high?
Yes. If the ratio is significantly higher than industry averages, it might mean you are losing sales because items are frequently out of stock, or you are incurring high shipping costs due to too many small restocks.
4. How often should I calculate this ratio?
Monthly or quarterly tracking is ideal for active businesses. This helps you spot trends early—if the ratio is declining month-on-month, it's a signal to investigate your purchasing or sales strategy.
5. Does this tool help with working capital management?
Directly. Efficient inventory management is the biggest lever to free up cash. A faster turnover means you get your money back from stock more quickly to pay other liabilities.
6. How is "Average Inventory" determined for seasonal businesses?
For seasonal businesses, taking just opening and closing stock for the year can be misleading. It is better to average the inventory levels at the end of each month (12 data points) for a more accurate result.
7. What is 'Stock Velocity'?
Stock velocity is another term for inventory turnover. It highlights the "speed" at which items move through your system—from the loading dock to the customer's hands.
8. Does the calculator account for sales tax or GST?
Internal business metrics like inventory turnover should always be calculated using values **Net of GST**. GST is a pass-through tax and should not be included in your inventory costs or COGS for efficiency analysis.
