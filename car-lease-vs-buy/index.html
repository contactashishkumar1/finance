How Corporate Car Leasing Saves Tax

Corporate car leasing, also known as the Salary Sacrifice model, is one of the most effective tax-saving tools for high-income earners in India. Instead of paying for a car EMI from your after-tax income (where ₹100 earned is only ₹70 in hand), the employer pays the lease rental from your pre-tax gross salary.

By doing this, you avoid paying income tax on the rental portion. For someone in the 30% or 35% tax slab, this results in a direct saving of nearly one-third of the rental cost. Additionally, these leases often bundle Insurance and Maintenance into the rental, allowing you to pay for these from your pre-tax income as well.

Deep-Dive: Perquisite Tax on Company Cars (2026)

When your employer provides a car for personal use, the Income Tax Department treats this as a benefit (Perquisite). For 2026, the tax logic remains based on cubic capacity (CC):

  • Engine < 1600 CC: ₹1,800 per month is added to your taxable income.
  • Engine > 1600 CC: ₹2,400 per month is added to your taxable income.
  • Chauffeur Provided: An additional ₹900 per month is added to your taxable income.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GST on car lease rentals refundable for employees?
No, individual employees cannot claim a GST refund on lease rentals. The 28% GST + Cess is a cost baked into your monthly rental quote. Only the employer can potentially claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) if the car is used for business travel.
What happens to the lease if I quit my job?
Typically, you have three options: 1. Transfer the lease to your new employer (Novation), 2. Early foreclosure by paying the outstanding principal + penalty, or 3. Return the car by paying a surrender fee.
How is Residual Value (RV) calculated in 2026?
Residual Value is the estimated market value of the car at the end of the lease term. In India, it usually ranges from 15% to 30%. A higher RV lowers your monthly rentals but increases the cost to buy the car at the end.
Which tax slab benefits most from corporate leasing?
The highest tax bracket (35.8% for 2026 high earners) benefits the most. Since you save tax at your marginal rate, a ₹1,00,000 rental effectively costs you only ₹64,200.
