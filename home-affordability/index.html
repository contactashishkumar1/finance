How is Home Affordability Calculated?

Indian banks use a benchmark called **FOIR** (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio), which usually caps your total monthly debt payments at 50% of your gross income.

Max EMI = (Gross Monthly Income × FOIR%) - Existing Debts

FOIR: Typically 50% for middle-income and 60% for high-income.

Max Loan: Present Value of the Max EMI over the given tenure and rate.

Affordable Price: Max Loan + Available Down Payment.

Mastering Your Home Buying Budget in 2026

Buying a home is the most significant financial decision for most Indian families. In 2026, with property prices rising and home loan interest rates fluctuating, the question isn't just "which home do I like?" but more importantly, "how much can I actually afford?" The Advanced Home Affordability Calculator provides a data-driven answer by mirroring the internal logic used by banks.

Most buyers make the mistake of looking at property prices first. Strategic buyers look at their cash flow first. By understanding your **FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)**, you ensure that your home purchase doesn't lead to "house poverty"—a state where you own a home but don't have enough money for lifestyle or emergencies.

The Role of Down Payment

The down payment is the most powerful lever in your affordability. While RBI guidelines allow banks to fund up to 80-90% of the agreement value (LTV - Loan to Value), having a larger down payment reduces your monthly EMI burden and lowers the total interest you pay over the tenure. In 2026, aiming for a 25-30% down payment is considered a healthy financial benchmark.

Home Affordability FAQs

1. How much monthly income is needed for a ₹1 Crore home?
Assuming an 8.5% interest rate, a 20-year tenure, and a 20% down payment (₹20L), you need a monthly take-home salary of approximately ₹1.5 Lakhs (with zero existing debts) to qualify for an ₹80 Lakh loan.
2. Does a good CIBIL score increase my affordability?
Indirectly, yes. A high CIBIL score (>750) helps you get lower interest rates. As interest rates decrease, your eligible loan amount increases for the same EMI capacity, thus increasing affordability.
3. Should I include registration costs in the affordability?
Yes. In India, stamp duty and registration add 5-8% to the price. Banks usually do not fund these. You should subtract these costs from your 'Available Down Payment' to find your true contribution capacity.
4. Is the FOIR always 50%?
No. For low-income groups, banks might cap it at 40%. For very high-income groups (earning >3 Lakhs/month), banks may allow an FOIR up to 65% because the remaining income is still sufficient for living costs.
5. Does rental income increase my affordability?
Yes, if you have other rental properties, banks add that income to your gross monthly income (usually at 70-75% value to account for vacancy), effectively increasing your loan eligibility.
6. Should I choose a 30-year tenure?
A longer tenure reduces your monthly EMI, which increases your borrowing power. However, it significantly increases your total interest payout. Most financial experts recommend a 20-year tenure as the optimal balance.
7. What happens if interest rates rise after buying?
For floating-rate loans, the bank will either increase your tenure or increase your EMI. Always keep a 10% buffer in your affordability calculation to absorb these shocks without straining your budget.
8. Does my age affect home affordability?
Yes. Banks usually require the loan to be repaid by age 60 or 65. If you are 45, you can only get a 15-year tenure, which leads to a higher EMI and lower affordability compared to a 25-year-old.
