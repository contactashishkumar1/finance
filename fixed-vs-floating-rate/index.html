Fixed vs Floating Rate: Which is Best in 2026?

In the Indian home loan market, the battle between fixed and floating rates is about Certainty vs. Savings. A Fixed Rate loan guarantees that your EMI will never increase, regardless of how high the RBI repo rate goes. This offers peace of mind for long-term budgeting.

Floating Rate loans are linked to an external benchmark. They usually start 1-2% lower than fixed rates. If market rates drop, your EMI decreases automatically. However, if rates spike, your tenure or EMI can increase significantly. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a fixed interest rate?
A fixed interest rate remains constant throughout the loan term, ensuring predictable EMIs.
What is a floating rate?
A floating rate changes according to market conditions and benchmarks set by the RBI.
Which is better for home loans?
Historically, floating rates have been cheaper for most Indian home loan borrowers over a 20-year horizon.
When to choose fixed?
Choose fixed if you believe interest rates are at an absolute bottom and are set to rise for the long term.
When to choose floating?
Floating is preferred when rates are high or when you want no prepayment penalties, which are common with floating loans.
RBI rate impact?
When the RBI repo rate rises, floating loan interest rates usually follow, increasing your cost of borrowing.
Can I switch from fixed to floating?
Yes, most banks allow you to switch for a small conversion fee. Use this calculator to see if the switch is worth the cost.

Strategic Summary

• Fixed rates offer payment stability but often come with a 1-2% interest premium.

• Floating rates take advantage of market dips but expose you to upward interest cycles.

• Review your loan strategy alongside your Income Tax benefits (Section 24b) to find the best net cost.

• Always check for reset clauses in "Fixed" rate contracts before signing.
