How is DTI Ratio Calculated?

DTI Ratio = (Total Monthly Debt / Gross Monthly Income) × 100

Total Monthly Debt: Sum of all EMIs, Rent, and Credit Card minimums.

Gross Monthly Income: Pre-tax monthly salary or business income. Check your Net Worth to see your full financial health.

Healthy Benchmark: Below 35% is considered Excellent by top Indian lenders.

Example Analysis (India)

If your gross salary is ₹1,00,000 and your monthly EMI is ₹35,00,000:
  • Total Monthly Obligations: ₹35,000
  • Calculation: (35,000 / 1,00,000) * 100
  • DTI Ratio: 35.00% (Healthy)

Why Your DTI Ratio is Your Financial Vital Sign

The Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is perhaps the most important percentage in your financial life after your credit score. In the 2026 Indian banking landscape, lenders use this ratio to determine your repayment capacity. If your DTI is too high, it signals to the bank that you are "over-leveraged"—meaning even a small financial setback could lead to a default.

The Indian Home Loan Benchmarks

Leading Indian banks like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI typically look for an FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio). Use our Savings Goal Tool to plan your down payment and keep your future DTI in check. While standard guidelines suggest 40%, high-income individuals earning over ₹2 Lakhs monthly might get approval for ratios up to 55-60%, provided their "Residual Income" is substantial. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio?
The Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is a personal finance measure that compares an individual's monthly debt payments to their monthly gross income.
What is a good DTI ratio for a home loan in India?
Most Indian banks prefer a DTI ratio of 40% or lower. A ratio above 50% is generally considered high risk for lenders.
