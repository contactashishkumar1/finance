How is Wealth Projected?

FW = L(1+i)n + P[((1+i)n-1)/i](1+i)

L: Initial Savings. Use the Lumpsum Tool to analyze one-time buys.

P: Monthly Contribution. Check your Monthly SIP efficiency.

i: Monthly ROI Rate (Annual / 12 / 100). Measure growth with the Compound Interest formula.

Wealth Projection Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Wealth Projection Calculator?
It is a financial tool that estimates the future value of your current savings and future monthly contributions based on a chosen rate of return and time period.
How does inflation affect wealth projection?
Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money. While your nominal wealth might be high, the real value tells you what that money can buy in today's terms.
Why is 12% ROI used as a standard for projection?
12% is a conservative long-term benchmark for diversified Indian equity mutual funds, accounting for management fees and market cycles.
How accurate are these wealth projections?
Projections are mathematical estimates based on constant rates. These numbers should be used as a goal-setting guide rather than a guaranteed bank balance.
What should my target inflation rate be for India?
A realistic assumption for a middle-class Indian lifestyle in 2026 is 6% to 7% p.a., accounting for education and healthcare costs.
Can I project wealth for a 30-year period?
Yes. Long-term projections highlight the massive impact of compounding, where your net worth grows most aggressively in the final decade.
Does this account for the new 12.5% LTCG tax?
The calculator provides pre-tax values. As of 2026, you should assume about 10-12% of your wealth gain will go toward taxes upon withdrawal.
Is real estate projection supported here?
Yes. If you assume your property will grow at 8% p.a., you can use that as the ROI rate for your lumpsum home value to project its future selling price.
